    Program Assistant (Tribunal Services Division)

In general the job was twofold; on one hand we handled telephone & email support, and drafted notices and memoranda for applicants and appellants making Freedom of Information requests, and on the other hand we provided clerical support to the mediators and adjudicators of the Tribunal Services Division. This usually entailed drafting and editing documents, as well as filing, faxing, and mailing.

  • Education
    Diploma :: Web Development (In Progress)
    College of the North Atlantic - St. John's, NL
    September 2016-June 2018

    To be honest, this is a pretty great program. I was expecting a heavier focus on a lot of the basic stuff I already knew; as I've been building sites since I was 12 or 13, my original idea in taking this program was more about getting a diploma to show that I know my stuff, I don't think I expected to learn quite as much as I did. Aside from providing a great foundation in OOP, the greatest strength of this program is bridging the gap between front-end and back-end development. A part of me feels like a third year with focus on more advanced frameworks and other tools (like AWS, Selenium, Angular etc..) would be great, but the program definitely gave me the skills to pick up new technologies easily on my own.

    Bachelor of Arts :: English Language & Literature

    September 2014-April 2016

    After moving back to Newfoundland from Toronto, I decided to head back to MUN and finish the degree I'd started way back in 2002. I changed my major from Philosophy to English, and knocked it out the new degree requirements in 2 years.     Diploma :: Paralegal Studies
    Seneca College of Applied Arts and Technology
    September 2010-April 2012

    Though I later decided that a career in law wasn't a good fit for me, this program was really valuable. Doing legal research and analyzing statutes, cases, and decisions requires a considerable amount of precision and a very critical eye. In a lot of ways programming and law are fairly comparable; writing a good contract requires the same broadly-scoped logic as constructing a program. In a court of law, as in a compiler, it's not the spirit of the document that matters: the specific words make a huge difference, and can make or break a good contract. I strongly believe the analytical skills I picked up in this program have had a dramatic impact on my programming skills.
